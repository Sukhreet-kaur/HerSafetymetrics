HerSafetyMatrics App

Uncovering Insights on Women's Safety: A Data-Driven Exploration

Analyzing women's crime data in India over two decades (2001-2020) is crucial for uncovering hidden patterns, regional disparities, and policy gaps that perpetuate gender-based violence. With rising incidents of rape, domestic abuse, and child exploitation, this dashboard empowers users to visualize trends, correlations, and state-wise insights, fostering informed advocacy and actionable reforms to build safer societies for women and girls.

In this app, I built an interactive dashboard to visualize and explore women's crime data in India from 2001 to 2020, using a cleaned CSV dataset with state-wise and district-level records. With Streamlit for the UI, Pandas for data handling, and Seaborn/Matplotlib for plots, I created a home page for dataset previews, filtering by state/year, and basic overviews like total cases trends via bar and line charts, plus correlation heatmaps and scatter plots across crime types. Users can then navigate to category-specific pagesâ€”Rape, Domestic Violence, POCSO, or Outdoor Crimesâ€”for deeper dives, including histograms for frequency distribution, line plots for year-wise trends, grouped bar charts by state or year, and multi-parameter comparisons with regressions. It's all about easy data exploration to spot patterns and disparities, without complex modeling, to help users understand and discuss crime trends intuitively.
